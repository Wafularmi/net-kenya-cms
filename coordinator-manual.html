1. Introduction — About This Portal

Welcome to the {{SCHOOL_NAME}} Coordinator Portal. As a Study Center Coordinator, you are the vital link between the college administration, the students in your region, and the trainers on the ground. This manual will help you understand every feature available to you in the system.

Your Role as a Coordinator

As a coordinator, you are assigned to a specific Region. Your access is scoped to the study centers and students within that region. You cannot see data from other regions. Your role badge in the system displays "Coordinator — {Region Name}" with a gold/yellow colour.

What You Can Do

  • Review and approve new student registrations
  • Manage student profiles and academic records
  • Record and monitor attendance
  • View grades and examination schedules
  • Track fee payments and financial summaries
  • Monitor student progress across courses
  • Manage support tickets
  • Moderate discussions in your region
  • Manage events, alumni, and certificates
  • Communicate via internal messaging

Device & Browser Requirements

The portal works on any device — phone, tablet, laptop, or desktop. Use any modern browser: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge.

Bookmark this URL: https://netfoundation.ke for quick access.

2. Logging In & Out

Your Login Credentials

Your coordinator account is created by the college administration. You will receive your username and password from the system administrator.

  • Username: Your email address or phone number (as set by the administrator)
  • Password: A password provided by the administrator (you can change it after logging in)

Step-by-Step: How to Log In

Step 1: Open your browser and go to https://netfoundation.ke
Step 2: Wait for the login screen to appear with Username and Password fields.
Step 3: Enter your username (email or phone number).
Step 4: Enter your password.
Step 5: Click "Sign In".
Step 6: You will see your Dashboard with your region name displayed at the top.
Passwords are case-sensitive. If you cannot log in, contact the system administrator.

How to Log Out

Step 1: Click your name at the top-right corner of the screen.
Step 2: Click "Logout" from the dropdown menu.

3. Dashboard — Your Command Center

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a quick overview of everything happening in your region.

Region Banner

At the top of the dashboard, you will see your region name displayed. This confirms which region's data you are viewing.

Pending Registrations Banner

If there are students waiting for approval, a prominent yellow banner appears at the top showing the count. Click it to go directly to the Pending Registrations screen.

Statistics Cards

Four cards at the top show key numbers for your region:

  • Total Students — All students in your region
  • Active Students — Currently enrolled students
  • Today's Revenue — Payments received today
  • Monthly Net — Net revenue for the current month

Dashboard Widgets

  • Recent Students — Latest 5 students enrolled in your region
  • Upcoming Events — Next 5 scheduled events
  • Finance Summary — Fee income, expenses, net balance, and students with outstanding balance
  • Attendance Alerts — Students whose attendance is below the minimum threshold
  • Tickets — Open and urgent support tickets
  • Online Users — Students from your region currently active on the portal

4. Managing Students

The Students screen lets you view, add, and manage all students in your region.

Viewing Students

Step 1: Click "Students" in the sidebar menu.
Step 2: A table lists all students in your region showing: Name, Admission Number, Email, Study Center, Program, Year, Status, and Fee Balance.
Step 3: Use the search bar to find a student by name, admission number, or phone number.
Step 4: Click the filter buttons to view students by status (Active, Inactive, Pending, All).

Adding a New Student

Step 1: Click the "Add Student" button above the student table.
Step 2: Fill in the student's details: Name, Email, Phone, Date of Birth, Gender, Study Center, Program, Academic Year.
Step 3: An admission number is auto-generated based on your school's format.
Step 4: Click "Save" to create the student record.
The student's login credentials (username = phone number, password = admission number) are automatically created when you add them.

Editing a Student

Step 1: Find the student in the table and click the "Edit" button.
Step 2: Update the student's details as needed.
Step 3: Click "Save" to apply changes.

WhatsApp Communication

From the student table, you can send WhatsApp messages directly:

Step 1: Click the "WhatsApp" dropdown next to a student.
Step 2: Choose from: Custom Message, Fee Reminder, Attendance Alert, or Welcome Message.
Step 3: The message is sent immediately via WhatsApp.

5. Pending Registrations — Approving New Students

When a student submits a registration request through the portal, it appears in the Pending Registrations screen for coordinators in their region to review and approve.

Viewing Pending Registrations

Step 1: Click "Pending Registrations" in the sidebar, or click the yellow banner on your Dashboard.
Step 2: A list shows all pending students with their name, phone, email, program, study center, and registration date.
Step 3: Click "Approve" on any student to begin the approval process.

Approving a Registration

The approval modal gives you full control over the student's details before finalizing:

Step 1: Review and edit the student's name, phone (username), email, program, and study center.
Step 2: The admission number is auto-generated based on your school's format (e.g., NET/KNSC/7-26/001).
Step 3: Choose a WhatsApp welcome template from the dropdown. A preview of the message appears below.
Step 4: Click "Customize" if you want to edit the welcome message, save it as a new template, or save changes to the existing template.
Step 5: Click "Finalize & Send Welcome" to approve the student.

What Happens After Approval

  • The student's status changes from pending to active
  • A user account is created (username = phone number, password = admission number)
  • A WhatsApp message with login credentials and welcome information is sent to the student
  • The event is recorded in the audit trail

Rejecting a Registration

Step 1: Click "Reject" next to the student's record.
Step 2: Confirm the rejection. The student record and any associated data are permanently deleted.
Rejection is permanent and cannot be undone.

6. Student Portal — Student Lookup Tool

The Student Portal is a powerful lookup tool that lets you view a complete profile of any student in your region.

How to Look Up a Student

Step 1: Click "Student Portal" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Search for a student by name, admission number, email, phone, or program.
Step 3: Click the student's name from the search results.

What You Can View

The student portal shows a consolidated view with these tabs:

  • Overview — Photo, name, admission number, program, average grade, attendance percentage, fee balance, quiz statistics
  • Courses — Enrolled courses with lesson counts, assessment counts, and video counts
  • Assessments — Available quizzes and exams with scores
  • Notes — Lesson resources and downloadable files
  • Grades — Grade history per course with scores and GPA
  • Finance — Fee summary, total paid, balance, payment history, and link to full statement
  • Attendance — Attendance percentage per course

Generating Transcripts

From the student portal, you can generate and view a student's transcript. The transcript includes all completed courses with grades and can be printed or saved as PDF.

7. Attendance Management

The Attendance screen lets you record and monitor attendance for students in your region.

Taking Attendance

Step 1: Click "Attendance" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Select the date and course for the session.
Step 3: A list of enrolled students appears. Mark each student as Present, Late, Absent, or Excused.
Step 4: Click "Save Attendance" to record the session.

Viewing Attendance Records

You can view attendance history for any course. Each student shows their total sessions, attended count, percentage, and a colour-coded status badge:

  • Green — 70% or above (good)
  • Orange — 50% to 69% (needs improvement)
  • Red — Below 50% (urgent attention needed)

Attendance Alerts

The Dashboard shows an attendance alerts widget listing students whose attendance falls below the minimum threshold. Follow up with these students to improve their attendance.

8. Grades

The Grades screen (if enabled by the administrator) lets you view grades for students in your region.

Step 1: Click "Grades" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Select a course from the dropdown to filter grades.
Step 3: A table shows each student's score, letter grade, GPA, and pass/fail status.
Grade data comes from quizzes and exams submitted by students. If a student has missing grades, check whether they completed the required assessments.

Grading Scale

  • A (80-100%) — Excellent — GPA: 4.0
  • B (70-79%) — Good — GPA: 3.0
  • C (60-69%) — Satisfactory — GPA: 2.0
  • D (50-59%) — Pass — GPA: 1.0
  • F (Below 50%) — Fail — GPA: 0.0
If the Grades option is not visible in your sidebar, it may have been disabled by the administrator.

9. Examinations

The Examinations screen (if enabled by the administrator) lets you view exam schedules for courses in your region.

Step 1: Click "Examinations" in the sidebar.
Step 2: View upcoming and past exams with details: title, course, date, time, and venue.
Step 3: You can see which students have registered for each exam.
Coordinators can view exam schedules but cannot create or modify exams. Contact the administrator for exam changes.

10. Finance & Fee Tracking

The Finance screen gives you visibility into fee payments and financial summaries for students in your region.

Viewing Financial Overview

Step 1: Click "Finance" in the sidebar.
Step 2: The dashboard shows: Today's collections, Monthly net, Total fee income, Other income, Expenses, and Overall net balance.
Step 3: Scroll down to see a list of students with outstanding fee balances.

Student Payment Details

From the Student Portal, you can view each student's full fee statement including:

  • Total fee charged
  • Total amount paid
  • Outstanding balance
  • Payment history with dates, receipt numbers, and methods (M-Pesa, bank, cash)
Use the WhatsApp option on a student's record to send fee reminders directly.

11. Progress Tracking

The Progress screen lets you monitor how students in your region are performing across their courses.

Step 1: Click "Progress" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Search for a student by name or admission number.
Step 3: View detailed progress per course including:
  • Overall progress percentage
  • Number of lessons completed
  • Number of quizzes passed
  • Attendance percentage per course
  • Pass rate for assessments

This is a valuable tool for identifying students who may be falling behind and need additional support.

12. Tickets — Support Management

The Tickets system lets you manage support requests from students in your region.

Viewing Tickets

Step 1: Click "Tickets" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Tickets are displayed with status filters: Open, In Progress, Resolved, Closed.
Step 3: Click a ticket to view its full details and conversation history.

Managing Tickets

Step 1: Open a ticket and read the student's issue.
Step 2: Type your response in the reply box and click "Send".
Step 3: Update the ticket status as you work on it (e.g., change from Open to In Progress).
Step 4: When resolved, mark the ticket as "Resolved" and the student will be notified.
The Dashboard shows a ticket widget with counts of open and urgent tickets that need your attention.

13. Discussions — Regional Moderation

The Discussions section lets you moderate course discussion forums for students in your region.

Step 1: Click "💬 Discussions" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Select a course to view its discussion thread.
Step 3: Only messages from students in your region are visible to you.
Step 4: You can post replies to answer questions or provide guidance.

Discussions are a great way to engage with students and address common questions that may benefit the entire group.

14. Internal Messaging

The Internal Messaging system is for communication between staff members and coordinators.

Step 1: Click "💬 Messages" in the sidebar (available for admins and coordinators only).
Step 2: Start a new conversation or reply to an existing one.
Step 3: Use this to coordinate with other staff, share updates, or escalate issues to the administrator.
Messages are internal only — students do not see this section.

15. Events Management

The Events screen lets you view and manage events for your region.

Step 1: Click "Events" in the sidebar.
Step 2: View upcoming and past events in a list or calendar view.
Step 3: Click "Add Event" to create a new event with title, date, time, description, and location.
Step 4: Events appear on the Dashboard for students in your region.

16. Alumni & Certificates

Managing Alumni

Step 1: Click "Alumni" in the sidebar.
Step 2: View a list of graduated students from your region.
Step 3: Update alumni contact information and track engagement.

Viewing Certificates

Step 1: Click "Certificates" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Browse the certificate history for students in your region.
Step 3: View issued certificates and reprint them if needed.

17. Chapel, Graduation, Hostel & Library

Chapel Attendance

Step 1: Click "Chapel" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Select the date and record attendance for students in your region.
Step 3: Chapel attendance is tracked separately from class attendance.

Graduation

Step 1: Click "Graduation" in the sidebar.
Step 2: View graduation eligibility and manage applications for students in your region.

Hostel Management

Step 1: Click "Hostel" in the sidebar.
Step 2: View hostel assignments and availability for your region's students.

Library

Step 1: Click "Library" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Browse the digital book collection available to all students and staff.

18. Reprinting Documents

The Reprint function lets you re-issue official documents for students in your region.

Step 1: Click "Reprint" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Search for the student whose document needs reprinting.
Step 3: Select the document type (certificate, transcript, etc.).
Step 4: Click "Reprint" to generate a fresh copy.
Only reprint documents when the original was lost or damaged. Duplicate documents are tracked in the system.

19. Troubleshooting — Common Problems

I Cannot Log In

  1. Check that Caps Lock is off. Passwords are case-sensitive.
  2. Make sure you are using the correct username (email or phone number).
  3. Clear your browser cache and cookies, then try again.
  4. If still stuck, contact the system administrator to reset your password.

The Page Shows Blank or Won't Load

  1. Press F5 or Ctrl+R (Windows) / Cmd+R (Mac) to refresh.
  2. Check your internet connection.
  3. Clear your browser cache.
  4. Try a different browser or an incognito/private window.

I Cannot See a Student's Data

  1. Confirm the student's study center is within your assigned region.
  2. Your access is scoped to your region only — you cannot see students from other regions.
  3. If a student was moved to a different region, contact the administrator.

A Feature Is Missing from My Sidebar

  1. Some features (Exams, Grades, Lessons) can be enabled or disabled by the administrator.
  2. Contact the system administrator if you believe a feature should be available.

An Approval Won't Go Through

  1. Check that all required fields are filled (name, phone, program, study center).
  2. Ensure the phone number is not already registered to another student.
  3. Make sure the student's study center belongs to your region.

Need More Help?

If your issue is not listed here, contact the system administrator through the Internal Messaging system or submit a ticket.
