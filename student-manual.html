1. Introduction — About This Portal

Welcome to the {{SCHOOL_NAME}} Student Portal. This online platform is designed to make your learning journey smooth, accessible, and effective. Whether you are studying from home, at a study center, or on the go, the portal puts everything you need right at your fingertips.

The Mission

This platform exists to ensure that no one has an excuse for ministering without having been trained. We believe that every person called to ministry deserves access to quality theological education. By blending face-to-face training with online learning, we make it possible for busy Pastors, Church leaders, and ministry workers to study at their own pace, from wherever they are, without leaving their calling or responsibilities behind.

What You Can Do Here

  • Access course materials and lessons anytime
  • Take quizzes and get instant feedback
  • Register for exams and view your schedules
  • Check your grades and download your transcript
  • Track your attendance record
  • View your fee statement and payment history
  • Read digital books from the library
  • Submit support tickets and get help
  • Participate in course discussions with fellow students and instructors

Device & Browser Requirements

The portal works on any device — phone, tablet, laptop, or desktop. Use any modern browser: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. You just need an internet connection.

Bookmark this URL: https://netfoundation.ke for quick access.

Navigating This Manual

Each section below explains one part of the portal in simple terms with step-by-step instructions. If you get stuck, check the Troubleshooting section at the end.

2. Logging In & Out

Your Login Credentials

When you are enrolled as a student, the college creates an account for you automatically. You will receive your login details from the administration or your study center coordinator.

  • Username: Your phone number (the number you gave during registration)
  • Password: Your admission number (e.g., NET/001/07-26/001)

Step-by-Step: How to Log In

Step 1: Open your browser and go to https://netfoundation.ke
Step 2: Wait for the login screen to appear. You will see two fields: Username and Password, plus a Sign In button.
Step 3: Type your phone number into the Username field.
Step 4: Type your admission number into the Password field. Be careful with capital letters and symbols — passwords are case-sensitive.
Step 5: Click "Sign In" (or press Enter on your keyboard).
Step 6: If your details are correct, you will enter the portal. If you see "Invalid credentials," double-check your username and password, then try again.
Important: Passwords are case-sensitive. "NET/001" and "net/001" are different. Make sure Caps Lock is off. If you still cannot log in, contact your study center coordinator or the college administration.

First-Time Login — Accepting Terms

The first time you ever log in, you will see a pop-up window with the college's Terms and Conditions. Please read them carefully. You must check the "I agree" box and click "Accept" to enter the portal. You will only see this once.

Forgot Your Password?

If you forget your password, contact the college administration or your study center coordinator. They can reset it for you. After reset, you will get a temporary password that you can change later from your profile settings.

How to Log Out

For security, always log out when you are done, especially on a shared device.

Step 1: Click your name or profile icon at the top-right corner of the screen.
Step 2: Click "Logout" or "Sign Out" from the dropdown menu.

3. Dashboard — Your Home Screen

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a quick summary of everything important.

What You Will See on the Dashboard

📊 Statistics Cards

Four cards at the top show numbers for your courses, exams, quizzes, and attendance. Click any card to jump directly to that section for more details.

📝 To Do List

A list of pending quizzes and upcoming registered exams, sorted by date. Items that are due soon appear first. Each item shows the course name and what you need to do.

📅 Upcoming Events

Important dates such as exam schedules and academic deadlines.

📢 Notifications

Alerts and messages from the college. The bell icon at the top-right also shows unread notification count.

📚 Your Courses

Quick links to your enrolled courses so you can jump straight into learning.

How to Navigate

Use the sidebar menu on the left side of the screen to move between sections. The sidebar lists all available areas:

  • Dashboard — This home screen
  • 🎓 My Hub — Your personal student dashboard (more details in section 4)
  • 📚 Courses — Your enrolled courses and lessons
  • 📋 Quizzes — Take and review quizzes
  • 📝 Exams — Register for exams
  • 📄 Grades — Your scores and transcript
  • 📖 Library — Digital books
  • 🎫 Support — Submit and track tickets
  • 💬 Discussions — Course discussion forums

The active section is highlighted. Click any section name to open it.

4. My Hub — Your Personal Dashboard

🎓 My Hub is a special dashboard just for you. It brings together everything about your studies in one beautiful page.

How to Open My Hub

Step 1: Click "🎓 My Hub" in the sidebar menu.
Step 2: The page loads with your name, admission number, program, and year displayed in a welcome banner at the top.

What You Will See in My Hub

Welcome Banner

Shows your name, admission number, program of study, and current year. A green "Live" indicator shows you are connected.

Quick Stats Cards

Four cards showing your enrolled courses count, registered exams, pending quizzes, and study notes. Click any card to jump to that section.

Tab Navigation

Below the stats you will find tabs that let you switch between different views:

  • Overview — A combined view of your upcoming exams, pending quizzes, and recent activity
  • 📚 Courses — List of all your enrolled courses with progress
  • 📝 Exams — Exam schedule and registration status
  • 📋 Quizzes — Pending and completed quizzes with scores
  • 📄 Notes — Study notes you have created for your courses
  • 💬 Discussions — Course discussion forums

Using the Tabs

Step 1: Click a tab name (e.g., "📚 Courses") to view that section.
Step 2: The content below the tabs changes to show the relevant information.
Step 3: Click another tab to switch views. The active tab is highlighted with a gold underline.
My Hub is the best place to start your day. Check it first to see what needs your attention — pending quizzes, upcoming exams, or new grades.

5. Courses & Lessons

Viewing Your Enrolled Courses

Step 1: Click "📚 Courses" in the sidebar.
Step 2: You will see a list or grid of all courses you are enrolled in. Each course card shows:
  • Course code (e.g., BTH101)
  • Course title
  • Instructor name
  • Credit hours
  • Progress bar showing how much you have completed
Step 3: Click "View Course" on any course to open it and see its full content.

Inside a Course

When you open a course, you will find:

Course Overview

A description of the course, its learning objectives, and the syllabus or topics covered.

Lessons

The course is divided into lessons. Each lesson covers one topic. Lessons are arranged in order — start from the first lesson and work your way through.

Lesson Materials

Each lesson may include different types of materials:

  • 📄 Study Notes — Written content explaining the topic
  • 🎥 Videos — Video lectures or teaching clips
  • 📎 Downloads — PDF files, slides, or other resources you can save
  • 📋 Quizzes — Short tests at the end of a lesson to check your understanding

How to Study a Lesson

Step 1: Open a course and click on a lesson title.
Step 2: Read the study notes or watch the video.
Step 3: Download any attached materials for offline review.
Step 4: Take the lesson quiz (if available) to test what you have learned.
Step 5: Mark the lesson as complete if there is a completion button.

Class Schedule

If your course has scheduled live classes, click the "Schedule" tab to view the timetable with dates, times, and venues or online meeting links.

6. Quizzes — Testing Your Knowledge

Quizzes are short tests that help you check how well you understand a topic. They appear within lessons or as standalone assessments for each course.

How to Take a Quiz

Step 1: Go to 📚 Courses and open the course that has the quiz.
Step 2: Find the quiz in the lesson content, or click the 📋 Quizzes tab inside the course.
Step 3: Click "Start Quiz" to begin. The quiz opens immediately.
Step 4: Read each question and select or type your answer.
Step 5: Use "Next" and "Previous" to move between questions.
Step 6: Click "Submit" when you have answered all questions.
Time Limit: Most quizzes have a time limit. A timer at the top shows remaining time. When time runs out, your answers are automatically submitted. You cannot pause or restart once begun.

After Submitting

Your score, percentage, and letter grade appear immediately. You will see which questions you got right and wrong, and for some quizzes, the correct answers are shown.

Retaking a Quiz

If the course allows retakes, a "Retake Quiz" button will appear after you see your results. Your highest score is usually kept as the final grade.

7. Exams — Formal Assessments

Exams are larger assessments that cover the full content of a course. They are scheduled on specific dates and times, and they contribute significantly to your final grade.

How to View Available Exams

Step 1: Click "📝 Exams" in the sidebar.
Step 2: You will see two lists:
  • Upcoming Exams — Exams in the future that you can register for
  • Past Exams — Exams that have already happened (for reference only)
Step 3: Each exam entry shows the title, course, date, time, and venue.

How to Register for an Exam

Step 1: Find the exam in the Upcoming list.
Step 2: Click "Register" next to that exam.
Step 3: A confirmation message appears. The button changes to a green "Registered" badge.
Step 4: The exam now appears in your registered list and on your Dashboard To Do list.
You can only register for exams of courses you are enrolled in. Register early to secure your seat.

On Exam Day

  • Check the date, time, and venue at least one day before
  • Arrive 15 minutes early
  • Bring your admission number and a valid ID
  • For online exams, ensure a stable internet connection
  • If you cannot attend, inform the administration as soon as possible

How to Request an Exam Retake

Step 1: Go to the Exams section and find the exam.
Step 2: Click "Request Retake" and give a brief reason.
Step 3: The administration will review and respond to your request.

8. Grades & Transcripts

How to View Your Grades

Step 1: Click "📄 Grades" in the sidebar.
Step 2: A table shows all your courses with:
  • Course — Name and code
  • Score — Your numerical score (out of 100)
  • Grade — Letter grade (A, B, C, D, or F)
  • GPA — Grade points earned (on a 4.0 scale)
  • Status — Pass or Fail

Grading Scale

  • A (80-100%) — Excellent — GPA: 4.0
  • B (70-79%) — Good — GPA: 3.0
  • C (60-69%) — Satisfactory — GPA: 2.0
  • D (50-59%) — Pass — GPA: 1.0
  • F (Below 50%) — Fail — GPA: 0.0

How to Download Your Transcript

Step 1: On the Grades page, click "View Transcript".
Step 2: A formatted transcript document appears on screen.
Step 3: Press Ctrl+P (Windows) or Cmd+P (Mac) to open print.
Step 4: Choose "Save as PDF" as the destination and click Save.
For an officially stamped transcript, contact the college administration office.

9. Attendance

How to Check Your Attendance

Step 1: Click "Attendance" in the sidebar (or find it inside your Courses section).
Step 2: For each course you will see:
  • Total Sessions — How many classes have been held
  • Attended — How many you attended
  • Percentage — Your attendance rate
  • Status — A color-coded badge

What the Colors Mean

  • Green — 70% or above (good)
  • Orange — 50% to 69% (needs improvement)
  • Red — Below 50% (urgent attention needed)
You need at least 70% attendance for each course. Falling below may trigger a follow-up from the administration.

If You Spot an Error

Contact your instructor or study center coordinator to correct your attendance record.

10. Fees & Payments

How to View Your Fee Statement

Step 1: Click "Finance" or "My Fees" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Your statement shows:
  • Total Fee — Full program fee charged
  • Total Paid — What you have paid so far
  • Balance — Remaining amount (total minus paid)

Payment History

A table lists every payment you have made with date, receipt number, amount, and payment method (e.g., M-Pesa, bank, cash). Keep your receipt numbers for your records.

Installment Plans

If you are on a payment plan, you will see each installment with its due date, amount, and whether it is paid, pending, or overdue.

Balance on Dashboard

Your outstanding balance also appears on the Dashboard. Green means fully paid; orange means there is an outstanding amount.

For fee questions, contact the finance office directly. Never make payments to individuals without official authorization.

11. Student Portal — Your Profile

The Student Portal section is where you manage your personal information and account settings.

How to Open Your Profile

Step 1: Click "Student Portal" in the sidebar (listed under your name or in the Main section).
Step 2: Your profile page opens with your personal and academic information.

What You Can Do Here

View Your Details

  • Personal: Name, admission number, phone, email, date of birth, gender
  • Academic: Program, year, study center
  • Fee: Current balance

Upload a Photo

Step 1: In your profile, find the photo section.
Step 2: Click "Choose File" and select a photo from your device.
Step 3: The photo will upload and display. It is used for your ID card.

Change Your Password

Step 1: Find the Change Password section in your profile.
Step 2: Enter your current password.
Step 3: Enter a new password and type it again to confirm.
Step 4: Click "Save" or "Update".
Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Do not share your password with anyone.

12. Library — Digital Books

The Library gives you access to digital books and study materials provided by the college to support your learning.

How to Use the Library

Step 1: Click "📖 Library" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Browse the list of available books. Each entry shows the title, author, and a short description.
Step 3: Click a book to open and read it in your browser.
Step 4: If a download option is available, you can save the book to read offline.

Searching

Use the search bar at the top to find books by title or author. Results filter as you type.

New books are added regularly. Check the library often for new materials.

13. Support, Tickets & Communication

Submitting a Support Ticket

A support ticket is how you ask for help or report a problem.

Step 1: Click "🎫 Support" or "Tickets" in the sidebar.
Step 2: Click "New Ticket".
Step 3: Choose a Category:
  • Academic — Questions about courses, assignments, or grades
  • Fee / Finance — Fees, payments, receipts
  • Technical — Portal problems, login issues, errors
  • General — Everything else
Step 4: Enter a subject and describe your issue in detail.
Step 5: Click "Submit".
Step 6: Track your ticket status (Open, In Progress, Resolved) from the Support page. You will be notified when there is an update.

WhatsApp Communication

The college may send you WhatsApp messages for:

  • 💰 Fee reminders
  • ⚠️ Attendance alerts
  • 👋 Welcome messages when you enroll
  • 📢 General announcements

You can also start a WhatsApp conversation from your profile by clicking the 📱 button.

Notifications (Bell Icon)

The 🔔 bell icon at the top-right shows a red badge with your unread notification count. Click it to see messages, alerts, ticket updates, grade postings, and announcements. Click any notification to view details.

Course Discussions

In the 💬 Discussions section, you can post questions, share insights, and reply to other students and instructors. Discussions are organized by course — select the course you want to join.

14. Troubleshooting — Common Problems

I Cannot Log In

  1. Check that Caps Lock is off. Passwords are case-sensitive.
  2. Make sure you are using your phone number as the username (with country code if outside Kenya).
  3. Your password is your admission number. Type it exactly, including all slashes and letters.
  4. Clear your browser cache and cookies, then try again.
  5. Try a different browser or an incognito/private window.
  6. If still stuck, contact your study center coordinator or submit a support ticket.

The Page Shows Blank or Won't Load

  1. Press F5 or Ctrl+R (Windows) / Cmd+R (Mac) to refresh.
  2. Check your internet connection. Try opening another website.
  3. Clear your browser cache (Chrome: Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data).
  4. Try a different device or network.
  5. If the problem persists, the server may be down for maintenance. Try again later.

A Quiz Will Not Submit

  1. Do not close the browser tab while taking a quiz. It may reset your progress.
  2. If the timer runs out, answers are auto-submitted.
  3. If "Submit" does not respond, try refreshing (may restart the quiz).
  4. If you have a technical issue during a timed quiz, contact support with the quiz name and time.

Grades or Attendance Are Missing

  1. Confirm you are enrolled in the course.
  2. Wait for the instructor to publish results — it may take time after an assessment.
  3. Refresh the page.
  4. If still missing, contact your instructor or submit a support ticket.

I Forgot If I Registered for an Exam

Go to Exams in the sidebar. Registered exams show a green "Registered" badge. You can also check the To Do list on your Dashboard.

Need More Help?

If your issue is not listed here, submit a support ticket from the portal (Support → New Ticket) or contact your study center coordinator.
