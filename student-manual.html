1. Introduction

Welcome to the NET Foundation College Student Portal. This portal gives you access to your courses, quizzes, exams, grades, attendance records, fee statements, and more — all in one place.

The portal works on any device with a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge). You can access it from your phone, tablet, or computer.

Bookmark the portal URL: https://netfoundation.ke for quick access.

2. Logging In

Your Credentials

When you are enrolled as a student, the college creates a login account for you. Your credentials are:

  • Username: Your phone number (the one you provided during registration)
  • Password: Your admission number (e.g., NET/001/07-26/001)

Steps to Log In

1. Open your browser and go to https://netfoundation.ke
2. You will see the login screen with fields for Username and Password.
3. Enter your phone number as the username and your admission number as the password.
4. Click "Sign In".
Passwords are case-sensitive. Type carefully. If you see "Invalid credentials," double-check your username and password.

First-Time Login — Terms & Conditions

The first time you log in, you will be asked to read and accept the college's Terms and Conditions. You must accept them to proceed. You will only see this once.

Forgot Your Password?

Contact the college administration or your coordinator to have your password reset. You can also ask your study center coordinator for assistance.

3. Your Dashboard

The Dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a quick overview of everything important:

  • 📊 Stats Cards — Shows your total courses, upcoming quizzes, registered exams, and attendance rate.
  • 📝 To Do — A list of pending quizzes and upcoming exams sorted by date.
  • 📅 Upcoming Events — Calendar of scheduled exams and important dates.
  • 📢 Notifications — Messages and alerts from the college.

Use the sidebar on the left to navigate to other sections of the portal.

4. Your Profile

Click on your name or avatar in the top-right corner to view your profile. Here you can:

  • View your admission number, program, year of study, and study center.
  • See your personal details (name, phone, email).
  • View your fee balance.
  • Change your password.
Keep your contact details (phone and email) up to date so the college can reach you.

5. Courses & Classes

The My Courses section shows all the courses you are currently enrolled in.

  • Each course card shows the course code, name, instructor, credits, and a progress bar.
  • Click "View Course" to open the course and see lessons, materials, and quizzes.
  • Click "Class Schedule" to see your weekly timetable.

Lessons & Materials

Inside a course, you will find lessons organized by topic. Each lesson may include:

  • 📄 Study notes and reading materials
  • 🎥 Video links
  • 📎 Downloadable resources (PDFs, documents)

6. Quizzes

Quizzes are short assessments that test your understanding of a topic.

Taking a Quiz

1. Go to My Courses and click "View Course" on the relevant course.
2. Find the quiz in the course content or go to the Quizzes section.
3. Click "Start Quiz" to begin.
4. Answer each question. Some quizzes are multiple-choice, others may require typed answers.
5. Click "Submit" when done. Your score will appear immediately.
Quizzes have a time limit. The timer starts when you begin. If you run out of time, your answers will be auto-submitted.

Quiz Results

After submitting, you will see your score, grade, and which questions you got right or wrong. You can retake some quizzes depending on the course settings.

7. Exams

Exams are formal assessments scheduled at specific dates and times.

Registering for an Exam

1. Go to the Exams section from the sidebar.
2. You will see a list of available exams for your enrolled courses.
3. Click "Register" next to each exam you want to take.
4. Your exam registration is confirmed when you see a "Registered" badge.

On Exam Day

  • Check the exam date, time, and venue listed in the Exams section.
  • Arrive at least 15 minutes early.
  • Bring your admission number and a valid ID.

Retaking an Exam

If you need to retake an exam, click "Request Retake" on the exam record. The admin will review and approve your request.

8. Grades & Transcripts

Viewing Your Grades

Go to My Grades from the sidebar. You will see a table of all your courses with your scores, letter grades, and GPA points for each course.

Your Transcript

Click "View Transcript" to see a formal document listing all your courses, scores, grades, and cumulative GPA. You can print this or save it as a PDF from your browser.

Grading Scale

The college uses the following grading scale:

  • A (80-100%) — Excellent
  • B (70-79%) — Good
  • C (60-69%) — Satisfactory
  • D (50-59%) — Pass
  • F (below 50%) — Fail

9. Attendance

The My Attendance section shows your attendance record for each course.

  • You will see the total number of sessions held, how many you attended, and your attendance percentage.
  • A green badge means good attendance (70% or above).
  • An orange or red badge means your attendance needs improvement.
Regular attendance is important. Students with attendance below 70% may be flagged for follow-up by the administration.

10. Fees & Payments

The Finance section shows your fee statement.

  • You can see your total fee amount, total paid, and outstanding balance.
  • Payment History lists all payments made with dates, receipt numbers, and amounts.
  • Installment Plan — If you are on a payment plan, you will see your installment schedule and due dates.

For any questions about fees or payments, contact the college finance office.

11. Library

The Library section gives you access to digital books and reading materials provided by the college.

  • Browse available books by title or author.
  • Click on a book to read it online or download it.

The library is updated regularly with new materials.

12. Support & Communication

Submitting a Ticket

If you have a question, issue, or complaint, go to Support from the sidebar and click "New Ticket". Select a category (e.g., Academic, Fee, Technical), describe your issue, and submit. The admin will respond to your ticket.

WhatsApp Communication

The college may send you WhatsApp messages for fee reminders, attendance alerts, and general announcements. You can also initiate a WhatsApp conversation from your profile or the communication center.

Notifications

The bell icon in the top-right corner shows your notifications. Click it to see messages, alerts, and updates from the college.

13. Troubleshooting

Can't Log In

  • Check that Caps Lock is off.
  • Ensure you are using your phone number as username (with the correct country code if applicable).
  • Your password is your admission number — type it exactly as shown on your enrollment documents.
  • If still stuck, contact your study center coordinator or the college admin.

Page Not Loading / Blank Screen

  • Refresh the page (F5 or Ctrl+R).
  • Clear your browser cache (Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data).
  • Try a different browser or an incognito/private window.
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection.

Quiz Not Submitting

  • Don't close the browser tab while taking a quiz.
  • If the timer runs out, answers are auto-submitted.
  • If you experience a technical issue, contact support with the quiz name and time.

Contact Support

For any issues not covered in this manual, submit a support ticket from the portal or contact the college administration directly.
